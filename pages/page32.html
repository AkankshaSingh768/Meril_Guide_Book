Abdominal Aortic Aneurysm (AAA)

An enlargement of the aorta in the abdomen that can lead to life-threatening rupture if untreated.

Anastomosis

The surgical connection between two structures, such as blood vessels or segments of the intestine.

Aneurysm Repair

Surgical procedure to correct an abnormal bulging or dilation of an artery.

Anticoagulation

The use of medication to prevent blood clot formation.

Aortic Arch

The curved portion of the aorta that supplies blood to the arms, head, and neck.

Aortic Endograft

A device used to temporarily occlude the thoracic or abdominal aorta during surgery.

Aortic Dissection

A serious condition where there is a tear in the aortic wall, causing blood to flow between the layers of the wall of the aorta.

Aortic Root

The section of the aorta closest to the heart where the aortic valve is located.

Aortic Valve

The valve between the left ventricle and aorta, regulating the flow of blood from the heart to the body.

Arteriotomy

Surgical incision into an artery to access or repair it.

Ascending Aorta

The portion of the aorta that rises from the heart and supplies blood to the head and upper body.

Atrial Fibrillation (AF)

An irregular and often rapid heart rhythm originating in the atria.

Atrial Septal Defect (ASD)

A congenital defect characterized by a hole in the wall between the left and right atria.

Calcification

The buildup of calcium deposits on vessel walls, leading to hardening and reduced function.

Cardiac Output

The amount of blood, in liters per minute, that the heart pumps; an indicator of cardiovascular health.

Cardiac Valve

A device in the heart that affects its size, shape, and function.

Cardioplegia

Cardiopulmonary Bypass (CPB)

A technique that temporarily takes over the function of the heart and lungs during cardiac surgery.

Chordae Tendineae

Thin, tendon-like strings that anchor heart valve leaflets to muscle surfaces.

Commissure

The point where the leaflets of a heart valve meet and connect.

Coronary Artery Bypass

A surgical procedure to bypass blocked arteries and restore blood flow to the heart muscle.
