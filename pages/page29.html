Popliteal Artery

The artery located behind the knee that supplies blood to the lower leg.

Radial Artery

The artery in the forearm commonly used for catheter access.

Rapid Exchange (RX) System

A system allowing quick exchange of devices over a guidewire during interventions.

Recanalization

Restoring flow of a blood vessel that was previously blocked.

Rotational Atherectomy

A technique using a rotating device to remove plaque from arteries.

Saphenous Vein

A major vein in the leg that can be affected by varicose veins or used in bypass surgery.

Semi-Compliant Balloon

A balloon that expands slightly beyond its nominal size, often used in vessel dilation.

Stent

The constant dilation of an artery by a metal or plastic mesh tube. Used to keep blood vessels open after treatment against disease or blockage.

Stent Deployment

The process of placing and positioning a stent in a blood vessel.

Stent Diameter

The width of the stent, which must be chosen to fit the target blood vessel.

Stent Endothelialization

The process of endothelial cells growing over a stent, integrating it with the vessel wall and preventing thrombosis or restenosis.

Stent Expansion

The increase in size of a stent to fit the vessel properly.

Stent Fracture

Breakage or cracking of a stent that can lead to loss of effectiveness or complications.

Stent Graft

A stent with a graft material used to reinforce blood vessel walls and repair aneurysms.

Stent Length

The total length of stent, which must be long enough to span the blockage or lesion in the vessel.

Stent Migration

Unintended movement of a stent from its initial position in the blood vessel.

Stent Radial Force

The force exerted by a stent to keep the vessel open after insertion.

Stent Restenosis

The recurrence of stenosis or vessel narrowing within a stent after treatment.

Stent Retriever

A device with which attachment can be made to remove a stent thrombectomy.

Superficial Femoral Artery (SFA)

The major artery in the thigh that supplies blood to the leg.
