Procedures implemented to ensure the accuracy and reliability of diagnos c tests.

Randomized Control Trial (RCT)

A study design in which participants are randomly assigned to receive either the intervention or a placebo/control.

Reagent

A substance used in a chemical reaction to detect, measure, examine, or produce other substances.

Reference Range

The range of values considered normal for a specific test in a defined population.

Regulatory Affairs

The management of processes related to ensuring that diagnostic tests comply with laws and regulations of different countries.

RNA Extraction

The process of isolating RNA from a biological sample.

RT-PCR (Reverse Transcriptase PCR)

A PCR technique that converts RNA into DNA for gene amplification, widely used for detecting RNA viruses.

Sample Preparation

The process of preparing a biological sample, including isolation, concentration, or purification, to make it suitable for analysis or testing.

Sensi vity

The ability of a test to correctly identify individuals with a disease (true posi ve rate).

Serology

The study of blood serum, particularly regarding immune responses and the presence of antibodies.

Southern Blot

A method used
to detect specific
DNA sequences
in a sample.

Specificity

The ability of a test to correctly identify individuals without a disease (true negatives).

Specimen Collection

The process of obtaining biological samples, such as blood, urine, or saliva, for diagnostic purposes.

Spectrophotometry

A technique that measures how much light a substance absorbs; how much light is absorbed at different wavelengths is a function of wavelength.

Stability

The ability of a sample or reagent to maintain its integrity under defined storage or usage conditions.

Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

A set of step-by-step instructions to ensure consistency and accurate laboratory practices.

Throughput

The number of tests or samples that can be processed by a diagnostic system in a given period.

Titer

The concentration of antibodies or virus in a sample, often measured by serial dilution.

Transport Medium

A solution used to preserve and stabilize specimens during transport to the laboratory.
