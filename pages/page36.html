Annuloplasty

Surgical repair of the valve annulus to restore proper valve function.

Anticoagulation Therapy

The use of medication to prevent blood clots after valve surgery.

Aortic Valve

The valve located between the left ventricle and the aorta, responsible for pumping oxygenated blood to the body.

Balloon Valvuloplasty

A minimally invasive procedure to widen a narrowed valve using a balloon.

Bileaflet Valve

A mechanical valve with two leaflets that open and close to regulate blood flow.

Biological Valve

A valve created from natural tissues, often from pig or cow heart tissue.

Biomechanical Performance

Assessment of how a valve prosthesis works in the body.

Bioprosthetic Valve

A valve derived from biological tissue, often used for valve replacement.

Bovine Pericardium

Tissue from cow heart used in the construction of bioprosthetic valves.

Cardiac Output

The volume of blood the heart pumps in one minute; critical in valve function.

Cardiovascular Imaging

Techniques used to visualize the heart and blood vessels, mainly for assessment.

Chordae Tendineae

Tendinous cords that anchor the valve leaflets to the heart muscles.

Commissures

The points where the valve leaflets meet, crucial for proper valve closure.

Coronary Sinus

A large vein collecting blood from the heart muscle, important during valve surgeries.

Durability Testing

Testing a valve prosthesis to ensure its long-term performance.

Echocardiography

A diagnostic tool using ultrasound to visualize heart structures, especially for valve assessment.

Ejection Fraction

The percentage of blood ejected from the left ventricle with each beat.

Endocarditis Prevention

Measures taken to prevent infection of the heart valves, especially after valve surgery.

Fluoroscopy

A real-time X-ray used during valve surgeries to guide the procedure.
