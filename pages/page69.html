Range of Motion (ROM)

The extent of movement possible in a joint or body part.

Real-Time Feedback

Immediate information provided to the surgeon during surgery for decision-making.

Recovery Time

The amount of time it takes for a patient to heal and return to normal function.

Registration Process

Process of aligning a patient’s anatomy to the robotic system for accurate guidance.

Rehabilitation Protocol

A set plan for recovery and physical therapy following surgery.

Robotic Arm

Mechanical arm that assists in surgery for enhanced precision and control.

Robotic Arm Trajectory

The planned path of the robotic arm during surgery to ensure accuracy, safety, and consistency.

Robotic Preoperative Assessment

Comprehensive assessment before robotic-assisted surgery to ensure correct position and controls.

Robotic-Assisted Surgery

Surgery performed with robotic assistance for increased precision and control.

Soft Tissue Balancing

Adjustment of surrounding tissues to ensure proper joint function post-surgery.

Soft Tissue Management

Techniques used to handle and protect soft tissues during surgery.

Software Update

Regular updates to robotic systems’ software to enhance functionality and security.

Sterile Field

Area in surgery that is maintained free of contaminants to prevent infection.

Subchondral Bone

Bone just below the articular cartilage in a joint.

Surgeon Console

The control interface where the surgeon operates the robotic system.

Surgeon Ergonomics

Design and arrangement of the operating room to enhance surgeon comfort and efficiency.

Surgeon Interface

The electronic device by which the surgeon interacts with the robotic system.

Surgical Accuracy

The precision with which a procedure is performed, often supported by robotics.

Surgical Workflow

The sequence of processes and tasks followed during robotics surgery.
