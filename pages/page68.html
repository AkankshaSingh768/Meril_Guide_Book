Joint Stability

The ability of a joint to maintain its position during movement.

Kinematics

Study of motion, particularly joint movement without considering forces.

Minimally Invasive Surgery (MIS)

Surgical techniques with smaller incisions to reduce recovery time.

Modular Implants

Implants that can be customized and adjusted during surgery for better fit.

MRI

Magnetic Resonance Imaging, a method that uses a magnetic field to visualize soft tissues, joints.

Operating Room Integration

The incorporation of robotics into the workflow and layout of the operating room.

Optical Tracking

Tracks joint regions using cameras and markers for real-time surgical navigation.

Partial Joint Replacement

Replacement of part of a damaged joint, while preserving the healthy portions of it.

Passive Robotics

Self-automated robotic devices or robots which act as positioning aids.

Patient Outcomes

The overall results and recovery experienced by the patient after surgery.

Patient-Matched Technology

Technology designed specifically to match a patient’s unique anatomy.

Patient-Specific Instrumentation (PSI)

Surgical tools customized for a patient’s specific anatomy.

Polyethylene Joint Insert

Plastic insert placed between joint components in joint replacement.

Postoperative Care

Care provided to the patient following a completed surgery.

Postoperative Evaluation

Assessment of surgical outcomes through examination and imaging.

Precision Cutting

Highly accurate bone cuts made by robotic systems during surgery.

Preparation

Preparative steps made using surgical tools before surgery and implant fixation.

Press-Fit

Implant fixation technique where the implant is tightly fitted into bone.

Prosthetic Alignment

Proper positioning of a prosthetic so the alignment is functionally and surgically favorable.

Radiopaque Markers

Markers visible on radiographic imaging to monitor position or fit.
