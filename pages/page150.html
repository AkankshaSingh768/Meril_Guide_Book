Subtrochanteric Nail

Nail used for fractures located below the trochanteric region of the femur.

Supracondylar Nail

Nail used to stabilize fractures above the condyles of the femur.

Tap

Suture used to create threads in divided or torn tissues.

Tension Band Plate

Plate used to counteract tensile forces and convert them into beneficial compressive forces at a fracture.

Tension Band Wiring

Technique that converts tensile forces into compressive forces to stabilize fractures.

Titanium Plate

Biocompatible metal plate for bone fixation due to its strength and corrosion resistance.

Biocompatibility

The ability of a material to perform without causing harm to living tissue.

Transverse Fracture

Fracture that occurs perpendicular to the long axis of the bone.

Unicortical Screw

Screw engaging only one bone cortex for fixation.

Variable Angle Plates

Plates allowing screws at different angles for better stability.

Weight Bearing

The ability of a bone or joint to support the body's weight during movement.

Wound Closure

Technique used to close a surgical incision or wound.
