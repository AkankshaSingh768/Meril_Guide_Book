Thrombectomy

A procedure to remove a blood clot from a blood vessel.

Thrombosis

The formation of a blood clot inside a blood vessel.

Tibial Artery

The arteries in the lower leg that supply blood to the foot.

Varicose Veins

Enlarged, twisted veins often found in the legs, caused by weakened vein walls or valves.

Vascular Access

The method of gaining entry into the vascular system for medical procedures.

Vascular Anatomy

The structure and arrangement of blood vessels in the body.

Vascular Aneurysm

An abnormal bulge in the wall of a blood vessel due to weakness.

Vascular Closure Device

A device used to close the puncture site after a vascular procedure.

Vascular Coil

A device used for embolization to occlude blood vessels or aneurysms.

Vascular Flow

The movement of blood through the vessels.

Vascular Guidewire

A flexible wire used to navigate and position other devices during vascular procedures.

Vascular Imaging

Techniques used to visualize blood vessels for diagnosis and planning.

Vascular Lumen

The interior space of a blood vessel through which blood flows.

Vascular Malformation

An abnormal development of blood vessels present at birth.

Vascular Occlusion

Blockage or closure of a blood vessel, impeding blood flow.

Vascular Remodeling

Changes in the structure and function of blood vessels in response to injury or disease.

Vascular Sheath

A short, hollow tube used to provide access to arteries or veins during procedures.

Venous Insufficiency

A condition where vein walls or valves cannot efficiently return blood to the heart, leading to swelling and discomfort.

Vessel Patency

The openness and unobstructed state of a blood vessel.
