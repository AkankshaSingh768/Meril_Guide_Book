Guide Catheter

A catheter used to direct other instruments to the target site within a blood vessel.

Guidewire

A thin, flexible wire used to guide other instruments through blood vessels.

Iliac Artery

The arteries that supply blood to the pelvis and lower limbs.

Inferior Vena Cava (IVC) Filter

A device placed in the inferior vena cava to prevent blood clots from traveling from the lower limbs to the lungs.

Intravascular Ultrasound (IVUS)

An imaging technique using ultrasound within blood vessels to visualize their structure.

Laser Atherectomy

A technique using a laser to vaporize plaque in blood vessels.

Liquid Embolic

Materials injected to occlude blood vessels and abnormal growths.

Magnetic Resonance Angiography (MRA)

An imaging technique using magnetic fields and radio waves to view blood vessels.

Microcatheter

A small, flexible catheter used to deliver devices or medication into tiny blood vessels.

Non-Compliant Balloon

A balloon that does not expand significantly beyond its nominal size.

Over-the-Wire (OTW) System

A system where devices are advanced over a guidewire for precise placement.

Percutaneous Access

Gaining entry to a blood vessel or cavity through the skin using a needle or catheter.

Percutaneous Transluminal Angioplasty (PTA)

A procedure to open blocked arteries using a balloon catheter.

Peripheral Artery Disease (PAD)

A circulatory disorder where narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to the limbs.

Peripheral Balloon

An inflatable balloon catheter primarily used in interventions for peripheral arteries and veins.

Peripheral Vascular Disease (PVD)

A disease of blood vessels outside the heart, often causing reduced blood flow to the limbs.

Plaque Debulking

The process of removing plaque from a blood vessel to improve blood flow before or after angioplasty or stenting.
