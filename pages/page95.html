Mirena

Intrauterine IUD used for contraception and menstrual management.

Oophorectomy

Surgical removal of one or both ovaries.

Ovarian Cyst

Fluid-filled sac on an ovary that may cause discomfort or other symptoms.

Ovarian Reserve

The number and quality of a woman's remaining eggs.

Ovarian Torsion

Twisting of the ovary which can interrupt blood flow and cause pain.

Ovary

Female reproductive organ that produces eggs and hormones.

Ovulation

Release of an egg from the ovary during the menstrual cycle.

Ovulation Prediction Kit

Device used to predict the timing of ovulation.

Pelvic Inflammatory Disease (PID)

Infection of the female reproductive organs.

Pelvic Organ Prolapse

Descent of pelvic organs from their normal position.

PMIC Ultrasound

Imaging technique using sound waves to view reproductive organs.

Perimenopause

Transitional period leading up to menopause characterized by hormonal changes.

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)

Hormonal disorder affecting women of reproductive age with irregular periods.

Postcoital Bleeding

Vaginal bleeding after sexual intercourse.

Postpartum

Refers to the period after childbirth.

Premature Ovarian Failure

Loss of ovarian function before age 40.

Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS)

Symptoms such as mood swings and bloating before menstruation.

Progesterone

Hormone that helps regulate the menstrual cycle.

Progestin

Synthetic form of progesterone used in hormonal therapies.

Reproductive Endocrinology

Medical specialty focusing on hormonal functions related to reproduction.

Salpingectomy

Surgical removal of one or both fallopian tubes.

Salpingoscopy

Examination of the fallopian tubes.

Spermicide

Chemical used to kill sperm and prevent pregnancy.

Sterilization

Permanent method of contraception through surgical or non-surgical means.

Subdermal Implant

Contraceptive device placed under the skin that releases hormones.
